## Angle
The news is a price, not a feature: generating a thousand images now costs a few cents. Break down what
a cost collapse like this really unlocks — and what it doesn't — for a solo creator or small business.
The no-hype job here is to separate "cheap enough to experiment freely" from "cheap enough to replace
your designer" (it isn't). A plain-English read on why price, not polish, is the story this week.

## Hook concept
Open on the jaw-dropping number — pennies for a thousand images — then pivot to "what does that
actually let you do?" so it's a meaning breakdown, not a spec dump. (Concept only.)

## Talking points
- The headline: fraction-of-a-cent image generation (Nano Banana 2 Lite, Gemini Omni Flash).
- What cheap-at-scale unlocks — testing lots of options, volume without a big bill.
- Who benefits most: solo creators and small teams, not just big studios.
- The honest limit: cheaper ≠ better; taste and direction still do the heavy lifting.
- Close on the shift: cost was the last barrier, and it's falling fast.
