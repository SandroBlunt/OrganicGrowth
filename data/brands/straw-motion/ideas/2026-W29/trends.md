# Trends — Straw Motion — Run 2026-W29

**Mode: curated sources.** `seed_pages` in `seeds.yaml` are Instagram/YouTube and not yet
scrapeable (Facebook is the only wired Apify platform — see issue #48), so this run pulled from the
Brand's two curated newsletter archives instead: [AI-Weekly](https://ai-weekly.ai/) and
[The Evolving AI Newsletter](https://newsletter.evolvingai.io/). Momentum below means **editorial
prominence in the source issue** (lead story vs. a smaller mention) — it is not a measured
over-performance metric, since there is no peer baseline in curated mode.

Sources checked, issues within the 7-day lookback (2026-07-06 to 2026-07-13):
- AI-Weekly: Issue 224 (2026-07-07) — a large weekly digest covering the week of July 1–7.
- The Evolving AI Newsletter: 6 daily issues, 2026-07-06 through 2026-07-13.

Only stories on this Brand's lane (agentic AI, generative-media workflows, model comparisons, how
the tech is evolving) were kept. Off-lane items seen in these issues and deliberately excluded:
the Musk/Altman personal feud, the Apple v. OpenAI lawsuit, the Midjourney v. Hollywood studios
copyright fight, Alibaba's Claude Code ban, and the bioterrorism-warning letter to Congress — all
real stories this week, but they're legal/geopolitical drama or heavy/dark subject matter that
doesn't fit Straw Motion's easy, confident, founder-style voice or its builder-focused niche.

## Ranked Trends

1. **Agentic AI moves into real day-to-day work** — momentum 0.95
   Three agent products for getting actual work done landed almost simultaneously: OpenAI's
   ChatGPT Work (lead story, Evolving AI 07-10), Anthropic's Claude Cowork (same issue), and Meta's
   Muse Spark 1.1. AI-Weekly's mega-digest independently corroborates the theme with a cluster of
   agent-workflow stories (Codex product workflows, agent operations management, agentic loops
   taxonomy). Strongest, most corroborated trend of the week — squarely in the "building
   agents/workflows" part of the niche.
   Hooks: "Your AI assistant just got a 9-to-5 job"; "Stop babysitting your AI — these tools finish
   your to-do list overnight."

2. **GPT-5.6 goes fully live for everyone** — momentum 0.90
   Lead story in Evolving AI's 07-08 issue: OpenAI ends limited preview and rolls GPT-5.6 out
   globally across three pricing tiers. AI-Weekly's digest independently references GPT-5.6 in
   several model-comparison items the same week.
   Hooks: "OpenAI's best model ever just went live for everyone"; "GPT-5.6 dropped today — the
   60-second version."

3. **Anthropic relaunches Fable 5 with a cheaper way to run it** — momentum 0.88
   A major story in Evolving AI's 07-08 issue (export controls lifted, plus a published pattern for
   getting "96% of Fable 5's results for 46% of the cost"), and by far the most repeated single
   thread in AI-Weekly's digest (8+ separate mentions across the week).
   Hooks: "Get 96% of the best model's power for less than half the price"; "Claude's flagship
   model is back — with a genuinely smart cost-cutting trick."

4. **Grok 4.5 beats Claude Opus 4.8 for half the cost** — momentum 0.85
   Lead story in Evolving AI's 07-09 issue: a direct, benchmarked model-vs-model comparison — exactly
   the "comparing models" lane of this Brand. Single-source this week but very strong editorial
   placement.
   Hooks: "Musk's new model just beat Anthropic's best, for half the price"; "Grok 4.5 vs Claude
   Opus 4.8 — the coding benchmark showdown."

5. **Anthropic finds a region in Claude that mirrors human reasoning** — momentum 0.75
   Lead story in Evolving AI's 07-07 issue (a "J-space" that collapses multi-step reasoning to near
   zero when removed); AI-Weekly's digest independently covers the same interpretability finding.
   Good "how the technology is evolving" story.
   Hooks: "Anthropic found the exact spot in Claude's brain where it thinks like us."

6. **X ships an all-in-one creator video editor** — momentum 0.55
   A secondary story in Evolving AI's 07-07 issue: green-screen recording + multilingual captions in
   one workflow. Relevant to the generative-media/creator-workflow lane, single source, mid-issue
   placement.
   Hooks: "Elon just built a green-screen editor straight into X — no cameras, no studio."

7. **Generative image/video models get dramatically cheaper** — momentum 0.40
   One item deep in AI-Weekly's digest: Google's Nano Banana 2 Lite ($0.034/1K images) and Gemini
   Omni Flash ($0.10/sec). Single source, not a headline item, but directly on-topic for the
   generative-media lane.
   Hooks: "$0.034 per 1,000 images — generative media just got absurdly cheap."
