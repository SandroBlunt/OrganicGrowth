## Angle
Google's Gemini Omni builds a personal avatar from a single selfie and a voice sample, and real-time tools
like Decart's Lucy 2.5 do live video style transfer with no editing bay. The tension for a creator: the
barrier to on-camera video just dropped to "a photo and a few seconds of your voice" — which is powerful
and a little unnerving. Break down what's newly possible and what to watch for, as news, not a tutorial.

## Hook concept
Open on the low bar that reframes video creation — one selfie plus one voice clip is now enough to make an
avatar that reads any script you give it (Gemini Omni). The surprise is how little it now takes. (Concept
only — writer lands the final line.)

## Suggested Recipe
Character Explainer with Cast (Reel, ~30-40s) — a generative-media story told by a confident on-camera
host mirrors the very tech it's covering.

## Talking points
- Google's Gemini Omni generates personal avatars from a single selfie and a voice sample (Google's
  official blog).
- Decart's Lucy 2.5 demos real-time video style transfer — changing a live video's look with no editing
  bay (Theoretically Media).
- Seedream 5.0 / Seedance 2.5 got stress-tested the same week, showing how fast generative video is moving
  (Theoretically Media).
- What it changes for a creator: on-camera-style video without a camera, studio, or edit — from a photo
  and a voice clip.
- The honest flip side: a face-and-voice avatar from one selfie also makes impersonation easier, so
  consent and disclosure matter.
