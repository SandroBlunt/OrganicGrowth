## Angle
A year ago almost nobody built on Chinese open models. Per one widely-shared market-share thread,
Qwen-based finetunes went from roughly 1% to 69% of the open-model market in twelve months. The tension:
the loud headline is "US vs China at the frontier," but the quiet reality is that a huge slice of the
world's actual building already runs on Chinese open weights. Show the builder what that shift means for
the tools they already use.

## Hook concept
Open on the single number that reframes the race — "1% to 69% in a year" — then reveal it's Chinese open
models quietly becoming the thing builders finetune on. The surprise is that the shift already happened
while the debate was still stuck on the frontier. (Concept only — writer lands the final line.)

## Talking points
- The chart claim: Qwen-based finetunes went from ~1% to ~69% of the open-model market in a single year
  (market-share thread by @DeanMeyerrr).
- Alibaba's Qwen3.8-Max is now rated "second only to Anthropic's Claude Fable 5" — the open line isn't a
  toy, it's near-frontier (South China Morning Post).
- The reframe: the frontier's top spot is one race; what people actually build on is a different one —
  and the second race already tipped.
- What it means for a builder: many tools and startups you use may already sit on Chinese open weights
  without ever saying so.
- The honest caveat: this is finetune share, not revenue or the single best model — a big footprint, not
  a crown.
