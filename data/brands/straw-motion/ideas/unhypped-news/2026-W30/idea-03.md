## Angle
Two labs, two math mysteries, one week: Claude Fable 5 produced a counterexample disproving the Jacobian
conjecture in three dimensions, and GPT-5.6 resolved a roughly 20-year-old open statistics problem from
a Wharton preprint. What makes this un-hype: unlike a flashy demo, a math proof is checkable — anyone can
verify it's actually right. So this isn't "trust the AI," it's "the AI did something we can confirm it
did." Explain why that's a different kind of milestone.

## Hook concept
Open on the reframe that separates this from usual AI hype: normally you have to take the AI's word for
it, but a solved math proof can be checked by hand — so this one is real whether or not you buy the hype.
Name the Jacobian conjecture up front. (Concept only — writer lands the final line.)

## Talking points
- Claude Fable 5 produced a counterexample disproving the Jacobian conjecture in three dimensions — a
  problem open in math for decades (36Kr).
- GPT-5.6 resolved a roughly 20-year-old open statistics problem drawn from a Wharton preprint by Edgar
  Dobriban.
- Why it's genuinely "unhypped": a math proof is verifiable — humans can check the answer, unlike a slick
  demo you just have to believe.
- Two different labs, two decades-old problems, in the same week — a pattern, not a one-off fluke.
- The honest limit: cracking a known hard problem isn't "AI is now a mathematician" — it's a tool finding
  results humans can confirm.
