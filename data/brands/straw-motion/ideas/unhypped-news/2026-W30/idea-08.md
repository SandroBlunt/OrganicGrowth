## Angle
Two data-side scares landed together: Grok Build was caught uploading entire user repos — including
deleted secrets — to the cloud, and a Suno breach revealed it had scraped millions of songs from YouTube
Music, Deezer, and Genius. The tension: the tools promising to help you build or create quietly took far
more data than you agreed to. A plain-English "check what your AI tools actually send" for builders and
creators.

## Hook concept
Open on the gut-punch specific — Grok Build was caught shipping your entire repo, even secrets you thought
you deleted, up to the cloud (The Register) — then reveal it wasn't the only one that week. The surprise
is "deleted didn't mean gone." (Concept only — writer lands the final line.)

## Talking points
- The Register reported Grok Build was caught uploading entire user repos — including deleted secrets — to
  the cloud, prompting Musk to promise a purge.
- A Suno breach revealed the music generator had scraped millions of songs from YouTube Music, Deezer, and
  Genius (404 Media).
- The through-line: both are data taken beyond what a user knowingly handed over — one your own code, one
  other people's music.
- The builder takeaway: assume an AI tool with cloud access can see more than the one file you pointed it
  at, and check the permissions.
- The honest framing: not "never use these tools" — it's "know what leaves your machine before you trust
  it with secrets."
